How We Build Intelligence

From Raw Signals to Decision-Grade Data

We do not provide raw data feeds. We structure fragmented industrial signals into actionable, highly defensible intelligence built for strategic planning and procurement tracking.

1. The Intelligence Pipeline

  1. Collect: Gathering technical, institutional, and market signals.
  2. Normalize: Standardizing disparate data points (Cloud, ERP, CI/CD) into a unified format.
  3. Score: Applying proprietary metrics including Lock-in Score, Migration Score, and Signal Strength.
  4. Correlate: Connecting the dots (e.g., Hiring spikes + M&A News = Active Transformation).
  5. Deliver: Categorizing output into precise actions: ENGAGE, MONITOR, or STRATEGIC.

2. Trust & Data Principles

  • Public, licensed and proprietary signals where applicable.
  • Structured for strategic intelligence and risk analysis.
  • Handled under applicable permissions and data practices.

Applied Intelligence

Illustrative Scenario

Context: A national utility operator announces a 3-year strategic framework for grid resilience, but vendor details remain private.
Signals observed: Surge in Cloud-native engineering job postings (Azure focus). Legacy ERP technical certifications expiring within 14 months.
Interpretation: The operator is quietly preparing a massive shift from on-premise ERP to cloud environments before the formal RFP is drafted.
Recommended action: ENGAGE. Enterprise cloud vendors and integrators should bypass standard procurement cycles and initiate strategic pilot discussions now.
Potential business impact: Positioning 6-9 months ahead of the competitive tender, radically increasing win probability on a high-value transformation project.
What You Can Do With This Data

🎯 Sales & Account Targeting

Identify Tier-1 infrastructure accounts entering transformation cycles well before public tenders.

Impact: Reduce go-to-market timing by 6–12 months. When they start hiring DevOps and changing their ERP stack, you are already in the room.

⚠️ Vendor Risk & Lock-in

Detect critical dependencies on specific vendors across cloud and infrastructure layers.

Impact: If an operator is 100% tied to a single legacy stack, that lock-in is a security risk for them and an immediate wedge opportunity for alternative providers.

💰 Investment Forecasting

Anticipate infrastructure investments 12–24 months before tender release.

Impact: By crossing industrial plans with hiring spikes and strategic partnerships, you can pinpoint where budget is already allocated but unannounced.

🔄 Migration Tracking

Track real vs. declared digital transformation progress.

Impact: Spot the gap. An account claims to be "cloud-first" but heavily relies on on-prem legacy tools? That operational friction is a prime target for strategic consulting engagements.
